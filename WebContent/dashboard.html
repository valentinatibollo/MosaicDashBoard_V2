Info
Pie charts show patient counts grouped by Demographic variables, BMI, Risk indexes at the last visit and Complication distribution.
Clicking on a chart section the temporal data mining algorithm extracts careflows and shows complication distributions of the selected group of patients.
Graphs show the most frequent temporal patterns of the population selected in the previous step. Applied selection criteria are shown in the 'Filters' section.
For further information on the specific patterns, please check the 'Info about...' link.
Pie charts show the complication distributions of the patients belonging to the selected step. The Histogram shows the distribution of the durations of the selected step in the temporal pattern. The bar chart shows the duration of the selected step for each patient. Applied selection criteria are shown in the 'Filters' section.
Histograms show the number of patients that have developed one of the listed complication before or after the first visit near the hospital.
The first graph 'First Complication' shows this information considering for each patient the first developed complication.
This use case aims at managing single patient during follow up visits. The dashboard depicts in a graphic way clinical information and behavioral patterns of clinical and administrative data.
Traffic lights show clinical variables and risk values profiles.
The color of the traffic light indicates if the variable was in range or not at the last follow up. The arrow indicates the trend between the last two follow ups.
Graphs show clinical variables, complications and risk profiles from the first visit near the hospital.
Scatter plots show the variables measures during follow ups. Time line plots show temporal abstractions. Weight temporal abstractions indicate if the measures Increase, Decrease or stay Steady. Time to target indicates that the patient has a 10%decrease of its weight in 6 months. Diet temporal abstraction indicate if the patient follow a good or bad diet regiment and for how long.
Graphs show the purchases of drugs during the disease evolution.
Drug purchases graphs indicate the number of days supply associated to purchases for a specific active principle. The grey box below the graph indicates whether the patient purchases larger or smaller quantities of the drug, compared to other patients that are treated with the same drug. Therapy purchasing behaviour graphs specify, with a 6 month granularity, the number of days with drug on hand divided by the number of days in each time interval. OVER labels indicate a value greater than 100%
